# Blog

Technical articles, research, CTF write-ups, and tool reviews. Written by the crew, for the crew.

## Our Philosophy

We do not publish for traffic. We publish for **record** and **transmission**. Every piece on this blog exists because someone in the crew solved a problem, discovered a technique, or mastered a concept—and wrote it down so the next person doesn't have to re-derive it from scratch.

**Quality criteria:**
- **Reproducible**: Code compiles, exploits run, detections fire
- **Educational**: The reader learns the *why*, not just the *what*
- **Honest**: Failures documented alongside successes
- **Current**: Outdated pieces are marked, updated, or removed

## Categories

### [Technical Articles →](/docs/blog/technical-articles)
Deep dives into vulnerabilities, protocols, and techniques. Written to teach. Every article starts from first principles and builds to practical application. We do not chase trends. We chase understanding.

### [Research →](/docs/blog/research)
Original findings, novel techniques, and academic paper summaries—when they meet our standard. We do not publish summaries of papers we have not implemented. We do not speculate on techniques we have not tested. Every research piece has been reproduced, extended, and validated in our environment.

### [CTF Write-Ups →](/docs/blog/ctf-writeups)
Step-by-step solutions. Not just flags—the methodology. Every write-up is a teaching artifact. If the reader cannot reproduce the exploit from our explanation, the write-up has failed.

### [Movie Night Write-Ups →](/docs/blog/movie-night-writeups)
Technical breakdowns of films we screen. What the film got right (and wrong). Not film criticism—technical autopsy. We separate the real from the fictional, then extract what teaches us.

### [Tool Reviews →](/docs/blog/tool-reviews)
Security tools. Productivity tools. Open source recommendations. Honest opinions. We do not review tools we have not used in production or real engagements. No press-release regurgitation. Every review reflects actual operational experience.

## Submission Process

1. **Draft** — Write in your learning pod. Get peer review.
2. **Validate** — Code runs. Exploit works. Detection fires. Benchmark reproduces.
3. **Edit** — Technical accuracy. Clarity. Structure. Citations.
4. **Publish** — Merge to `main`. The Refinery indexes it. The crew reads it.
5. **Maintain** — If a technique becomes obsolete, you update the post. Your name stays on it.

## Standards

- **Teach, don't just show** — Explain the *why*, not just the *how*
- **Include code** — Reproducible snippets, not screenshots
- **Cite sources** — Link to RFCs, CVEs, papers, vendor advisories
- **State prerequisites** — What the reader needs to know first
- **No marketing language** — "Game-changing," "revolutionary," "best-in-class" → delete

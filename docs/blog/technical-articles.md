# Technical Articles

Deep dives into vulnerabilities, protocols, and techniques. Written to teach.

## Our Approach

We publish when we have something that changes how the reader thinks — not just a writeup of a known vulnerability. Every article starts from first principles and builds to practical application. We do not chase trends. We chase understanding.

## Article Structure

Every technical article follows this structure:

1. **Thesis** — One sentence. What you will understand by the end.
2. **Prerequisites** — Explicit. What the reader must know first. Links to prerequisite nodes.
3. **Ground Truth** — The specifications, manuals, RFCs, or source code that anchor the derivation.
4. **Derivation** — Step-by-step from ground truth to the vulnerability/technique. No "because." Only "spec says X, therefore Y."
5. **Demonstration** — Working code. Minimal, complete, compilable. Not screenshots.
6. **Mitigation & Detection** — How to fix. How to detect. Sigma/YARA/Snort rules where applicable.
7. **Implications** — What this enables. What it breaks. What comes next.
8. **References** — Primary sources only. No blog-spam chains.

## Writing Guidelines

- **Teach, don't just show** — Explain the *why*, not just the *how*
- **Include code** — Reproducible snippets, not screenshots
- **Cite sources** — Link to RFCs, CVEs, papers, vendor advisories
- **State prerequisites** — What the reader needs to know first
- **No marketing language** — "Game-changing," "revolutionary," "best-in-class" → delete

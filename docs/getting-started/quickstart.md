# Getting Started

Welcome to Resolute Raccoon. This guide orients you — whether you're a prospective member, a visitor exploring what we do, or a friend of the club wanting to understand the lay of the land.

## What This Is

Resolute Raccoon is a **cybersecurity club** founded at Southern Delta University, Ozoro, Nigeria. We are not a company. We are not a training provider. We are a group of people who decided that scattered tutorials and outdated resources were killing our progress — so we built our own structure.

We operate on two tracks:

| Track | Focus | For Whom |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **School of Cybersecurity** | Technical mastery — binary exploitation, web security, reverse engineering, cryptography, cloud, hardware | Anyone willing to do the work |
| **School of Life** | Character formation — discipline, health, relationships, finance, leadership, communication | Every member (mandatory) |

We reject the lie that you must specialize in one thing. A Cybersecuritist who cannot cook is fragile. One who cannot speak is invisible. One who cannot manage money is a vulnerability. We build exceptional humans who happen to be exceptional hackers.

## How We're Organized

### Spaces (The Curriculum)
Our technical curriculum lives in **[Spaces](/docs/spaces)** — three domains:

1. **[Learning Hub](/docs/spaces/learning-hub)** — The core technical pathway. Curated from the world's best free platforms (pwn.college, TryHackMe, PortSwigger), structured into locked progression paths with validation gates.
2. **[Skills Development](/docs/spaces/skills-development)** — The supporting pillars: Cooking, Videography, Public Speaking, Deep Technical Communication. Technical skill without these is a narrow blade — sharp but brittle.
3. **Specialized Tracks** — Bug Bounty methodology, Research process, and emerging domains.

### Hacker Club (The Operations)
Where theory meets target. **[Hacker Club](/docs/hacker-club/overview)** runs:
- **Movie Nights** (weekly) — Dissect real breaches and films that teach
- **Hackathons** (quarterly) — 48-hour pressure vessels against custom infrastructure
- **Conferences & Webinars** — Attend as crew, present our research
- **The Raccoon Report** (weekly) — Intelligence brief by operators, for operators

### Learnings (The Deep Dives)
Five focused domains for compressed, high-density study: **[Mathematics for Programmers](/docs/learnings/mathematics-for-programmers)**, **[Clean Code](/docs/learnings/clean-code)**, **[Java for Beginners](/docs/learnings/java-for-beginners)**, **[Readers Club](/docs/learnings/readers-club)**, and **Mathematics for Cybersecurity** (coming Q2 2026).

### Features (The Infrastructure)
The tools that make it work: **[Curated Learning](/docs/features/curated-learning)**, **[Locked Progression](/docs/features/locked-progression)**, **[Refinery AI](/docs/features/refinery-ai)**, **[Thumbnail Cards](/docs/features/thumbnail-cards)**, **[Progress Tracker](/docs/features/progress-tracker)**.

## For Visitors

You're welcome at **Movie Nights** and **Webinars**. No membership required. Just show up ready to engage. The discussions are technical, fast, and assume familiarity with the primary sources.

**Hackathons, internal channels, Learning Hub validation, and Progress Tracker** are members-only. You earn your way in.

## How to Become a Member

Membership is not "joining." It's **earning your way in**.

### The Path
1. **Complete Foundations** — Linux Fundamentals, Networking Basics, Web App Basics in the Learning Hub (validated)
2. **Pass the Diagnostic** — 30-minute practical: Linux, networking, coding, debugging. No studying. Just shows where you are.
3. **Pick a Track** — Red, Blue, Purple, Research. Your learning pod (3–5 people) forms around it.
4. **Show Up** — Movie Nights. Hackathons. Webinars. Newsletter contributions. The club runs on participation.

### The Standard
- **No spectators.** If you're in the room, you're on a team. You have a role. You deliver.
- **No anonymity.** Real names. Real faces. Real accountability.
- **No shortcuts.** You don't get the exploit until you understand the vulnerability. You don't get the detection rule until you've seen the attack.
- **Legal only.** Every target authorized. Every scope defined. Every action logged.

## What You Need

- A Linux environment (WSL2, VM, or bare metal)
- Basic programming comfort (any language)
- Willingness to derive, not memorize
- Willingness to teach, not just learn
- Internet access for weekly syncs

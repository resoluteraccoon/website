# Movie Nights

## Why Movies?

We believe film has a unique way of impacting the mind. Each session has a *why* behind it — a reason it earns its place in our curriculum. Cinema compresses decades of history, strategy, and human drama into two hours. It lets us witness the consequences of decisions we haven't made yet. It lets us study the anatomy of failure and the architecture of breakthrough without paying the price ourselves.

There is historical value here. The Social Network isn't just a movie about Facebook — it's a case study in the psychology of builders, the toxicity of ambition, the cost of moving fast and breaking things. Mr. Robot isn't just a show about hackers — it's a portrait of dissociation, the fragility of reality, the cost of seeing the strings. The Matrix isn't just sci-fi — it's an allegory for awakening, for the moment you realize the system you've trusted is a construct. Citizenfour isn't just a documentary — it's the real-time capture of a man deciding that truth matters more than safety. Ex Machina isn't just AI fiction — it's the Turing test inverted, where the machine tests the human.

We curate these films using state-of-the-art criteria: historical significance, technical accuracy, strategic depth, and philosophical weight. We don't pick movies because they're popular. We pick them because they teach.

## The Breakdown

After watching, we dissect. We pause at the moments that matter. We analyze each scene — the hacks, the strategies, the social engineering, the tradecraft. We separate the real from the fictional. But we also ask: *what fiction has inspiration we can bring to reality?*

Could the social engineering in The Social Network actually work? What would a real pentest of Facebook's early infrastructure look like? Is the malware in Mr. Robot plausible? How would a real AI alignment problem manifest? We don't just watch. We reverse-engineer.

## Year 1: Foundations & Awakenings

**Month 1 — The Social Network**  
Attention economy and manipulation. How platforms engineer addiction and what that teaches us about social engineering.

**Month 2 — Citizenfour**  
Surveillance and whistleblowing. The real cost of truth in a world built on secrets.

**Month 3 — The Great Hack**  
Data weaponization and democracy. Cambridge Analytica, psychographic targeting, electoral integrity.

**Month 4 — Zero Days**  
Nation-state cyber warfare. Stuxnet, cyber-physical attacks, attribution dilemmas.

**Month 5 — We Are Legion**  
Hacktivism and collective action. Anonymous, operations Payback, Tunisia, Sony, ethics of vigilantism.

**Month 6 — Terms and Conditions May Apply**  
Privacy erosion. Corporate data harvesting, policy loopholes, consent theater.

**Month 7 — Deep Web**  
Darknet markets and anonymity. Silk Road, Tor, crypto, law enforcement infiltration.

**Month 8 — The Internet's Own Boy**  
Open access and prosecutorial overreach. Aaron Swartz, CFAA abuse, academic liberation.

**Month 9 — Hacking Democracy**  
Election infrastructure vulnerability. Diebold machines, memory card exploits, paper trail necessity.

**Month 10 — Code 2600**  
Hacker history and culture. Phone phreaking → BBS → early internet; community evolution.

**Month 11 — TPB AFK**  
Copyright wars and distributed resilience. The Pirate Bay trial, legal vs. technical enforcement.

**Month 12 — In the Realm of the Hackers**  
Teen prodigies and systemic failure. Mendax, Prime Suspect, Australian telecom breach, juvenile justice.

**Month 24 — The Next Frontier**  
Emerging paradigms. Space systems, bio-cyber, neurotech, autonomous agents.

## How Movie Nights Work

1. **Watch** — Full documentary/movie. No interruptions. Absorb the story.
2. **Pause** — Key moments get paused. We break down what just happened technically.
3. **Debate** — Could it have been prevented? What would you have done differently?

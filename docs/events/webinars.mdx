# Webinars

## Why Webinars

A conference is a firehose. A webinar is a scalpel.

Monthly. Focused. Deep. One topic. One expert. Two hours.

We run webinars because some topics demand sustained attention — not a 25-minute slot between hallway conversations. Some speakers cannot travel. Some topics are too niche for a general conference track. Some techniques are too new for a CFP cycle.

## Format

### Structure
- **15 min** — Context & threat landscape (why this matters now)
- **60 min** — Technical deep dive (live demo, code walkthrough, architecture)
- **30 min** — Q&A (no moderator filter — direct questions)
- **15 min** — Internal debrief (members only: what do we implement?)

### Recording & Archive
Every webinar is recorded, transcribed, and indexed. The recording enters the Refinery within 24 hours. Members who missed it can watch at 2x speed and still inject insights.

## Topics We Cover

### Technical Deep Dives
- **eBPF Internals & Security** — Tracing, XDP, CO-RE, verifier bypass
- **Kernel Exploitation Modern** — KPTI, SMAP/SMEP, KASLR, KPTI bypasses
- **Cloud-Native Attack Paths** — Kubernetes RBAC, container escapes, supply chain
- **Hardware/Firmware** — UEFI, SMM, Intel ME, AMD PSP, BMC
- **Cryptography Engineering** — Implementation flaws, side channels, post-quantum
- **AI/ML Security** — Prompt injection, data poisoning, model extraction, defenses

### Strategic & Operational
- **Detection Engineering** — Sigma rule design, false positive reduction, coverage mapping
- **Threat Intelligence Operations** — Collection, enrichment, scoring, sharing (MISP, OpenCTI)
- **Incident Response at Scale** — Automation, playbooks, cloud forensics
- **Secure Architecture** — Zero trust, microsegmentation, secrets, policy as code
- **Career & Ethics** — Bug bounty economics, responsible disclosure, clearance, ethics

## How We Select Speakers

We do not take volunteers. We hunt.

**Criteria:**
- Has published novel research or tooling in the last 12 months
- Can demonstrate live, not just present slides
- Willing to share code/configs/scripts after the session
- Available for follow-up questions in our internal channel

**Sources:**
- Conference speakers we couldn't catch live
- Tool authors (Ghidra, Frida, Volatility, Nuclei, Sigma, etc.)
- Bug bounty hunters with novel techniques
- Academic researchers with practical implementations
- Industry practitioners solving problems at scale

## Member Obligations

**Attendance is not optional.** If you are in the club, you attend.

**Pre-work:** Read the speaker's last 3 papers/talks. Prepare 3 questions.

**Post-work:** Within 48 hours, inject 1 insight into the Refinery. Run a 10-minute teaching session for your learning pod.

# Public Speaking

## The Standard: Can a Non-Technical Audience Understand?

Every member speaks. This is not optional.

We do not do conventional public speaking. No "three tips for confidence." No "imagine the audience in their underwear." We do real work.

You present on technical security matters. Your research ([Research](/docs/spaces/research)). Your bug bounty success ([Bug Bounty](/docs/spaces/bug-bounty)). A CTF strategy you developed. A vulnerability you found. A tool you built.

The test: Can a non-technical person understand what you did, why it mattered, and how you did it?

If they cannot, you failed. Not the audience. You.

## The Format

We invite people. Technical and non-technical. Currently we simulate, but the standard is real.

You have 20 minutes. No slides unless they serve the explanation. Demos are live. Code is shown. Questions are answered.

Topics you might present:

- **Your Research** — What you discovered. The gap in existing literature. Your methodology. Your results. The implications.
- **Bug Bounty Success** — The target. The reconnaissance. The vulnerability class. The bypass. The proof of concept. The payout. The lessons.
- **CTF Strategy** — The challenge. The category. The insight that unlocked it. The tools you wrote. The time pressure.
- **Tool Development** — The problem. Why existing tools failed. Your architecture. The edge cases you handled. The benchmarks.
- **Incident Analysis** — A real breach you dissected. The kill chain. The missed detections. The lessons for defenders.

## The Standard: Non-Technical Comprehension

After your talk, we ask a non-technical person: "What did they do? Why did it matter? How did they do it?"

If they cannot answer all three, you redo it. Not "good enough." Not "close enough." You redo it until they understand.

This is not cruelty. This is the job. A Cybersecuritist who cannot explain their work to the people who pay for it, the people who use the systems protect, the people who write the laws — is a liability.

## The Feedback Loop

After every talk, you get:

1. **Technical Review** — From peers who know the domain. Accuracy. Depth. Novelty.
2. **Clarity Review** — From non-technical audience. Did they understand? Where did they get lost?
3. **Delivery Review** — Pacing. Voice. Presence. Handling of questions.

You incorporate. You redo if needed. You grow.

## Why This Matters

We are building Cybersecuritists who can walk into a boardroom, a classroom, a village hall, a legislative hearing — and make security make sense.

Not "security awareness." Real understanding. The kind that changes budgets. The kind that changes laws. The kind that changes how systems are built.

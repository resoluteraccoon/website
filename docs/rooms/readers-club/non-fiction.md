# Non-fiction

General non-fiction that expands how you think. History, science, philosophy, psychology, economics — the context that makes you a better engineer and a clearer thinker.

**History & Biography**  
*The Making of the Atomic Age* (Rhodes) — Science, politics, and the birth of the nuclear era.  
*The Code Breakers* (Kahn) — Cryptology from ancient ciphers to DES.  
*Skunk Works* (Rich) — How Lockheed built the U-2, SR-71, and F-117 in secrecy.  

**Science & Technology**  
*The Structure of Scientific Revolutions* (Kuhn) — Paradigm shifts, normal science, how fields evolve.  
*Chaos* (Gleick) — Nonlinear dynamics, sensitive dependence, the geometry of nature.  
*The Information* (Gleick) — From talking drums to Shannon entropy to quantum information.  

**Psychology & Decision Making**  
*Thinking, Fast and Slow* (Kahneman) — System 1 vs System 2, biases, prospect theory.  
*Sources of Power* (Klein) — Naturalistic decision making. How experts decide under pressure.  
*The Art of Thinking Clearly* (Dobelli) — 99 cognitive errors, concise and actionable.  

**Economics & Systems**  
*Antifragile* (Taleb) — Things that gain from disorder. Convexity, optionality, barbell strategy.  
*The Black Swan* (Taleb) — Unpredictable, high-impact events. Narrative fallacy, silent evidence.  
*Seeing Like a State* (Scott) — Why grand schemes fail. Legibility, high modernism, metis.
